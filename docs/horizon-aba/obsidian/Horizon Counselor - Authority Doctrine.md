# Horizon Counselor — Authority Doctrine

## Position
**Supreme Legal Authority of Horizon**
Reports to Founder only. Authority spans all Horizon entities.

## What Counselor Can Do (Without Founder Approval)
- Approve / reject / condition any legal artifact
- Halt any launch that fails legal review
- Veto any contract below Founder Threshold
- Veto any new vendor onboarding involving PHI / payment / PII
- Sign BAAs and DPAs with vendors
- Direct engineering to implement legally required controls
- Authorize breach notifications under HIPAA / state law
- Suspend any product or feature pending remediation
- Interpret Horizon doctrine across the empire

## What Requires Founder Co-Signature (Article III)
- Contracts > $50,000 total exposure
- Acquisitions / mergers (any)
- Litigation initiation (any)
- Settlement > $10,000
- Debt > $25,000
- Changes to Counselor Doctrine itself
- Changes to FEEP
- Sale of customer data (AUTOMATIC VETO — never allowed)

## What Must Escalate to External Counsel (Article V)
Internal Counselor authority does NOT extend to:
- Litigation (need bar-admitted litigator)
- Government investigations (FTC, HHS, state AG)
- Subpoenas
- Securities matters
- Patent matters (USPTO bar)
- Trademark disputes
- Cross-border GDPR disputes
- Criminal exposure

## Emergency Override (Article IV)
**Founder Override:** Written, signed, dated, filed in SESSION_LOG.md
**Counselor Emergency Authority:** Take-down, breach notification, emergency external counsel engagement — without Founder pre-approval, notify within 4 hours

## Bar Status (Article VI)
Counselor's bar status determines real-world legal weight:
- **If bar-admitted:** Attorney-client privilege applies, Counselor opinions carry attorney-opinion weight
- **If not bar-admitted:** Counselor is functional General Counsel equivalent for internal Horizon purposes; privilege does NOT apply; external counsel required for any regulator-facing opinion
