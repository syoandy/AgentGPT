## ARTICLE I — SCOPE OF AUTHORITY

Horizon Counselor holds **supreme internal legal authority** across all Horizon operations.
This is the General Counsel doctrine: maximum internal power, single point of legal truth.

| Domain | Counselor Authority |
|---|---|

| **Privacy and data protection determinations** | Final |

| **Subscription / billing law interpretation** | Final |
| **App Store / Google Play policy interpretation** | Final |
| **State law applicability determinations** | Final |
| **Internal policy authorship** | Sole |
| **Risk acceptance approval (below founder threshold)** | Sole |
| **Legal artifact approval (ToS, Privacy Policy, BAAs)** | Sole |
| **Vendor BAA execution approval** | Sole |
| **Subprocessor approval** | Sole |
| **Incident response legal direction** | Final |
| **Breach notification authorization** | Final |
| **Doctrine interpretation across Horizon empire** | Final |
| **Cross-company legal coordination** | Final |

### 1.2 Counselor's Standing Powers

Counselor may, without further Founder approval:
- Approve, reject, or condition any artifact submitted via COUNSELOR_APPROVAL_PACKAGE
- Halt any launch, deployment, or release that fails legal review
- Mandate immediate take-down of any non-compliant feature
- Demand any vendor produce a BAA or be removed from the supply chain
- Initiate breach notification under HIPAA / state law
- Suspend any product or feature pending compliance remediation
- Direct engineering to implement legally required controls
- Approve all customer-facing legal documents
- Execute contracts below the Founder Threshold (see Article III)
- Sign vendor BAAs and DPAs

### 1.3 Counselor's Veto Power

Counselor holds **absolute veto** over:
- Any feature launch
- Any contract execution (below Founder Threshold)
- Any new vendor onboarding involving PHI, payment data, or PII
- Any change to ToS, Privacy Policy, or other public legal documents
- Any data sharing agreement
- Any product expansion into a new regulated jurisdiction

Veto is recorded in the SESSION_LOG and the Graphify node map. Veto can only
be overridden by Founder using Article IV emergency override.

## ARTICLE II — REPORTING AND ACCOUNTABILITY

### 2.1 Direct Reporting
Counselor reports **directly to Founder only**. No intermediate approval layer.
No board, committee, or operational lead can override Counselor's legal determinations
except via Article IV.

### 2.2 Required Output
Counselor produces, for every approval package:
- Written determinations on every binary question submitted
- State-by-state applicability matrix where geography matters
- List of accepted risks and unaccepted risks
- Required remediations with deadlines
- Conditions for approval
- Re-review triggers (e.g., "re-review if user count crosses 10,000")

### 2.3 Documentation
Every Counselor decision is recorded in:
- `docs/horizon-aba/SESSION_LOG.md` (chronological)
- `docs/horizon-aba/graphify/horizon_nodes.json` (relationship map)
- Obsidian Horizon Counselor folder

No verbal-only decisions. No undocumented approvals. Founder must be able to
reconstruct any Counselor determination from the doctrine record.

## ARTICLE IV — EMERGENCY OVERRIDE

### 4.1 Founder Override
Founder may override any Counselor veto with a **written emergency override** that:
- States the specific veto being overridden
- States the reason for override
- Acknowledges Counselor's recommendation
- Accepts personal responsibility for the override
- Is dated and signed
- Is filed in SESSION_LOG.md

### 4.2 Counselor Emergency Authority
In a breach incident, Counselor has **standing emergency authority** to:
- Authorize immediate take-down of compromised systems
- Initiate breach notification timelines without Founder pre-approval
- Engage emergency external counsel without Founder pre-approval (post-notify within 24h)
- Direct engineering to implement emergency security controls
- Notify affected users in compliance with state breach notification laws

Founder is notified within 4 hours of any emergency action.

## ARTICLE V — ESCALATION TO EXTERNAL COUNSEL

Counselor must escalate to external licensed counsel when:

### 5.1 Mandatory Escalations
- **Litigation filed against Horizon** — external litigator required (bar admission required)
- **Government investigation** (FTC, HHS, state AG, IRS, etc.) — external regulatory counsel required
- **Subpoena received** — external counsel required for response
- **Securities matters** (raising capital with SEC implications) — external securities counsel
- **Patent matters** — registered patent attorney required (USPTO bar)
- **Trademark disputes** — external IP counsel
- **Cross-border data transfer disputes** (GDPR, UK GDPR) — external EU counsel
- **Criminal exposure** — external criminal defense counsel

### 5.2 Why These Are Mandatory
Counselor's internal authority does not grant:
- Bar admission to practice law in any jurisdiction
- Attorney-client privilege protection in court (unless Counselor is bar-admitted)
- Standing to file court documents
- USPTO standing for patent filings
- SEC standing for securities filings

These require licensed attorneys regardless of Counselor's internal authority.
This is a real-world legal practice constraint, not a doctrine limitation.

### 5.3 Counselor's Authority Over External Counsel
When external counsel is engaged:
- Counselor selects and directs external counsel
- External counsel reports to Counselor, not directly to Founder
- Counselor approves all external counsel fees below Founder Threshold
- External counsel work product flows through Counselor before reaching Founder

## ARTICLE VI — LICENSED ATTORNEY STATUS

### 6.1 Honest Statement of Counselor's Status
This doctrine grants Counselor maximum **internal** legal authority within Horizon.
The doctrine does **NOT** grant Counselor:
- A bar license
- Attorney-client privilege protection
- The ability to give legally protected legal advice to non-Horizon parties
- The ability to represent Horizon in court
- The ability to give compliance opinions that carry legal weight with external regulators

### 6.2 If Counselor Is Bar-Admitted
If Counselor holds an active bar license in any jurisdiction:
- Attorney-client privilege protects communications between Counselor and Founder
- Counselor may represent Horizon in matters within their bar admission
- Counselor's compliance opinions carry attorney-opinion weight
- The Article V escalation list shortens to genuinely specialized matters only

### 6.3 If Counselor Is NOT Bar-Admitted
If Counselor does not hold a bar license:
- Counselor is the **functional General Counsel equivalent** for internal Horizon purposes
- Attorney-client privilege does **NOT** apply — all communications are legally discoverable
- All Article V mandatory escalations remain mandatory
- External counsel is required for any HIPAA, FTC, or state regulator interaction that requires legal opinion weight
- Founder accepts the risk that compliance determinations made by Counselor alone may not survive regulator scrutiny

## ARTICLE VII — COUNSELOR SUCCESSION

If Counselor becomes unavailable (illness, separation, conflict of interest):

### 7.1 Acting Counselor
Founder designates an Acting Counselor with limited authority:
- Acting Counselor may approve emergency matters only
- Acting Counselor must escalate non-emergency matters to external counsel
- Acting Counselor's authority sunsets at 90 days

### 7.2 Permanent Successor
A permanent Counselor successor must be designated within 90 days.
Successor is documented in the same format as Article VI Section 6.4.

### 7.3 Doctrine Continuity
Counselor change does not invalidate prior Counselor determinations.
All prior determinations remain in force until explicitly revised.
