# Empire Scalability Score
**Parent doctrine:** FEEP Master  
**Cadence:** Quarterly + on entity changes  
**Reviewer:** Founder + Chief of Staff

This score measures how ready the empire is to scale — to add more entities, more revenue, more complexity — without creating chaos.

---

## 1. SCORING METHODOLOGY

Five dimensions, each scored 0–20 points. Total: 0–100.

| Dimension | Max Points | Question |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Doctrine Coverage | 20 | Is every operational decision covered by written doctrine? |
| 2. Automation Depth | 20 | Are routine tasks automated? Do humans only handle judgment calls? |
| 3. Counselor Independence | 20 | Can Counselor operate without daily Founder input? |
| 4. Entity Isolation | 20 | Can one entity fail without taking down others? |
| 5. Truth Score Discipline | 20 | Do production claims match production reality? |

**Total Score = sum of all 5**

Interpretation:
- 90–100: Empire-ready. Can comfortably scale to 5+ entities.
- 70–89: Functional. Can scale to 2–3 entities with effort.
- 50–69: Founder is the bottleneck. Scale carefully, fix bottlenecks first.
- < 50: Survival mode. Adding entities will cause collapse.

## 10. WHY THIS SCORE MATTERS

This score answers one question:

**"If Yoandy OS got hit by a bus tomorrow, how long until the empire collapses?"**

- Score 90+: Months. Counselor + Entity Leads + doctrine carry the operation. Founder succession kicks in.
- Score 70–89: Weeks. Significant disruption, but recoverable.
- Score 50–69: Days. Most decisions are still in Founder's head.
- Score < 50: Hours. Founder is the only thing holding it together.

The whole point of FEEP is to drive this score up over time so the empire survives — and scales — beyond any one person.
